# Hackathon Timeline

*(in Pacific Time)*

## May 7 - Official Launch

Track reveal, prize pool announcement, and opening of the hackathon will be announced during Sui Live in Miami

## May 7-June 21 - Building Period

Active building phase

## June 21 - Submission Deadline

Final deadline for all project submissions. After the deadline, you can continue to make changes to your project but it may not be reflected in the shortlisting process

## July 8 - Shortlisted Teams Announcement

Shortlisted teams announced. The shortlisted teams will present live on Demo Day.

## July 20-21 - Demo Day

Shortlisted teams will present their projects live virtually to our panel of judges

Final winners announced and will be invited to pitch during Sui Basecamp 2026.

## Award Structure

Prizes for Sui Overflow 2026 will follow a **split distribution model** designed to encourage continued development beyond the hackathon:

- **50% of the prize** will be awarded upon announcement of winners
- **50% of the prize** will be awarded after successful mainnet deployment

If a winning team has already deployed their project to mainnet by the time winners are announced in August, they will receive **100% of the prize upfront**.

## Notes

- Mainnet deployment must meet the minimum functional requirements as defined by the Sui team and/or track sponsors.
- Teams are encouraged to continue building post-hackathon to unlock the full prize amount.
- Additional support, opportunities, and ecosystem pathways may be available for top-performing teams.

# Submission Checklist

Before submitting your project, please ensure you have:

| Fields Needed | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Project Name | Clear + simple |
| Description | What it does, why it matters |
| Project Logo | 1:1 ratio (JPG/PNG) |
| Public GitHub Repo | Required to be public during judging period |
| Demo Video | Required (YouTube preferred, ≤ 5 min) |
| Website | Optional, highly recommended |
| Deployment | Testnet or Mainnet |
| Package ID | If deployed on-chain |

<aside>

# Judging Criteria

Core track projects will primarily be evaluated based on:

## Product & UX - 20%

Quality, usability, polish, and overall user experience.

## Real-World Application - 50%

Meaningful problem-solving, market relevance, and long-term value.

## Technical Implementation - 20%

Technical quality, reliability, and meaningful integration with Sui.

## Presentation & Vision - 10%

Clarity of presentation, storytelling, and long-term vision.

</aside>

<aside>

# What Makes a Strong Overflow Project?

Strong projects typically:

- solve meaningful problems
- have polished UX
- leverage Sui meaningfully
- demonstrate strong product thinking
- show long-term potential beyond the hackathon

Overflow is focused on meaningful products and ecosystem impact, not just technical demos.
